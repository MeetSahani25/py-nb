# The Decision Framework
## From 200 weekly stocks → 5-10 conviction positions

---

## The honest problem first

You will have 50-200 stocks showing up every week. Many are genuinely good companies. 
You cannot research all of them deeply. You cannot hold all of them.
The data system you've built is a **train spotter** — it tells you which trains are moving.
But you still have to decide which one to board, with how much money, and when to get off.

Most people fail not at finding good stocks but at:
1. Holding too many (diluting returns, can't track all)
2. Sizing too small on conviction (makes no difference even if right)
3. No exit plan (hold through thesis break because "it's a good company")
4. Paralysis from too much choice ("analysis paralysis")

---

## The funnel — 5 stages from 200 to 5

### Stage 1 — Automatic filter (system does this already)
**200 stocks → ~40-50**

Your screener + silent horse scoring already does this.
Gold/Silver from silent horse = pre-filtered universe.
Everything below Silver = ignore entirely.

Auto-disqualify without looking further:
- PSU / government-owned
- Commodity-margin dependent (GRM, steel spread, rubber)  
- PE above 80x with less than 30% growth
- Promoter pledge above 20%
- OBV divergence warning present
- Stage 3 risk badge

**Output: ~40-50 Silver + Gold stocks**

---

### Stage 2 — The thesis test (30 minutes, once a week)
**40-50 stocks → ~15-20**

For each Silver/Gold stock, answer ONE question in one sentence:
*"Why is this stock going to be worth more in 12-24 months than it is today?"*

If you cannot answer it in one sentence — skip it.
If the answer is "it's going up" — skip it.
If the answer is vague ("good company, good sector") — skip it.

Valid one-sentence theses:
- "New plant going live next quarter will double revenue, currently priced on old capacity"
- "Data center boom means their HVAC products have 3-year order visibility, not priced in yet"
- "PLI incentive adds 300bps margin improvement, market hasn't noticed"
- "Promoter buying steadily, FII just started entering, earnings accelerating 3 quarters in a row"

This single filter eliminates most stocks instantly.
You are looking for a **specific, falsifiable reason** the stock will be higher.

**Output: ~15-20 stocks with clear theses**

---

### Stage 3 — The 3-question deep dive (1-2 hours per stock)
**15-20 stocks → ~8-10**

For each stock that passed Stage 2, answer exactly 3 questions:

**Q1: Is the thesis intact right now?**
Check: latest quarterly results, any management commentary, any news that changes the story.
If the thesis depends on a plant going live — has it? On FII buying — is it continuing?
A thesis can break before a stock falls. Catch it early.

**Q2: What is the bear case and how bad is it?**
Every stock has a bear case. Write it down.
If the bear case is "commodity prices fall" — that's uncontrollable, reduces conviction.
If the bear case is "execution risk on new plant" — that's monitorable, acceptable.
If you cannot articulate a bear case — you don't understand the stock well enough yet.

**Q3: What is the specific trigger that will make the market notice?**
Silent horses are great but they need a catalyst to become visible.
Next quarterly results? Plant commissioning? Government order announcement? FII disclosure?
If there's no visible trigger in the next 3-6 months — it may remain silent longer than you want.

**Output: ~8-10 stocks that pass all 3 questions**

---

### Stage 4 — The conviction scoring table
**8-10 stocks → ranked order**

Score each stock 1-3 on 6 dimensions:

| Dimension | 1 (weak) | 2 (ok) | 3 (strong) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Thesis clarity | Vague | Specific | Specific + falsifiable |
| Earnings momentum | Flat/declining | Growing 15-30% | Growing 30%+ QoQ |
| Management quality | PSU/unclear | Family-run, ok track | Skin in game, low pay, buying |
| Technical setup | Stage 3/extended | Stage 2 mid-run | Stage 1/recovering |
| Catalyst visibility | None in sight | 3-6 months | Next quarter |
| Downside protection | >40% max loss | 20-40% max loss | <20% max loss |

**Minimum score to invest: 12/18**
**Score 15-18: high conviction, larger position**
**Score 12-14: medium conviction, smaller position**
**Score below 12: watchlist only, don't invest yet**

Any dimension scoring 1 = mandatory discussion with yourself. 
Two or more 1s = skip regardless of total score.

**Output: ranked list of 8-10 with conviction scores**

---

### Stage 5 — Position sizing
**Ranked list → actual portfolio**

This is where most people make the biggest mistake.
They find a great stock and put 2% of capital in it.
Even if it doubles, it barely moves the portfolio.

Professional framework (Kelly-inspired, simplified):

**High conviction (score 15-18):** 8-12% of portfolio
**Medium conviction (score 12-14):** 4-6% of portfolio  
**Watchlist (score below 12):** 0% — just watching

**Hard rules:**
- Maximum 10 positions at any time
- No single stock above 15% of portfolio
- No single sector above 30% of portfolio
- If you can't sleep thinking about a position — it's too large

**The math:** 8 positions at 8-12% average = 80-100% invested.
This is concentrated enough to matter, diversified enough to survive.

## The watchlist — your pipeline, not a graveyard

Most people's watchlists are graveyards — stocks they looked at once, added, forgot.
Yours should be a **living pipeline** with 3 buckets:

### Bucket 1 — Ready to buy (5-10 stocks)
Stage 5 complete. Conviction score 12+. Just waiting for:
- Better entry price (stock pulls back 5-10%)
- Catalyst confirmation (results due, plant commissioning)
- Position size available (selling something else first)

These get price alerts on Zerodha/TradingView. You act within days, not weeks.

### Bucket 2 — Building conviction (10-20 stocks)
Passed Stage 2 (have a thesis) but still completing Stage 3-4.
Monitoring quarterly results, news, FII data.
These move to Bucket 1 when thesis strengthens, or get dropped when it weakens.

### Bucket 3 — On radar (20-30 stocks)
Silver/Gold from silent horse report. Not researched deeply yet.
Reviewed once a month when new silent horse report comes in.
Promoted to Bucket 2 if they keep appearing and thesis becomes visible.
Dropped if they stop appearing or OBV divergence appears.

## The sell discipline — the hardest part

Most frameworks cover buying. Nobody talks enough about selling.
You need pre-defined exit rules BEFORE you buy, not after.

**Sell immediately (no discussion):**
- Thesis breaks (plant delayed indefinitely, PLI revoked, fraud discovered)
- Promoter pledge appears or increases above 15%
- Two consecutive quarters of declining revenue (not one — one can be seasonal)
- FII holding drops more than 3% in a single quarter
- OBV divergence appears on a stock you're holding

**Consider selling (review needed):**
- Stock hits your 12-24 month price target early (trim 50%, let rest run)
- Better opportunity exists and you're at position limit (upgrade vs hold)
- Silent horse score drops below Silver for 3 consecutive weeks (fading signal)
- RSI above 75 + price 30%+ above EMA200 (take some profits)

**Never sell because:**
- Market fell broadly (if thesis intact, this is a gift — add)
- Stock hasn't moved in 3 months (silent horses take time)
- You're nervous (that's what position sizing is for — sleep at night)
- Someone on Twitter/Telegram said sell (check your thesis instead)

## The most important thing

You will miss stocks. Good ones. Ones that ran 100%.
That's fine. There will always be more trains.

What you're building is not a system to catch every move.
It's a system to catch the high-conviction ones where you understand the thesis,
size correctly, and hold through the noise.

One KRN-quality pick per quarter, sized at 10% of portfolio, held for 12 months =
transformational returns. You don't need 20 KRNs. You need 3-4 per year.

The goal is not to be busy with stocks.
The goal is to be right about a few, and size them properly.
